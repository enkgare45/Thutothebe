Thoughts of a pessimist on a rainy day.

Posted on 4 Jan by Emmanuel Nkgare

Looking out this window with drops of rain water trickling on it. A sudden flash of light sparks in the distance. I cover my ears in a futile attempt to smother the anticipated thunderous sound which when it finally strikes, proves to have been overestimated. As soon as this happens, a thought is planted in my mind. A rather weird thought.

I wonder, why doesn't lightning ever strike close to me? I mean really close to me...like on this tree out this window. It always strikes far off into the distance and almost all the time, I donâ€™t even know what's been struck! It wouldn't be so bad if lightning could strike that big SUV in my neighbour's yard while I watch, and with little cars next to it would be a cherry on top. He likes to nag about it to me, ridding him of it would be killing two birds with one pebble!

On the contrary, on top of the temporarily blinding flash, I would probably be subjected to a profoundly deafening sound of which i am highly liable to sustain a permanent hearing impairment. Pondering upon the implications, the damage far outweighs the rewards. Therefore in conclusion, I'd rather enjoy it from afar.

Oh look, a flash, probably struck at the local butchery! They sold me sub-standard meat last week. Serves them right!

Inspired by George Carlin's way of thought in "Jammin' in New York" show

These buds

Posted on Jan 5

These buds are the reason why I have no thoughts no more

These buds are the reason why my thoughts lack depth

These buds are a barrier between I and my surroundings, I can’t hear birds sing anymore

For without them my soul and the soul of the earth resonate in unison

These buds prevent me from being with myself

For as long as I’m with them, I’m never alone.

When loneliness comes creeping on

I long for the days when I wasn’t slave to these buds

But then remember; life was so simpler then
